essayist | geographer | cartographer

I grew up outside of Richmond, VA and currently reside in Lexington, KY. In addition to pursuing my Geography PhD at the University of Kentucky, I also write fiction and nonfiction.

research interests

I became interested in geography accidentally, as most geographers seem wont to do, during my first semester in college. Throughout my undergraduate career, I was interested in the racialization of cultural landscapes in the US. Since entering graduate school, my focus has shifted towards the intersections of race, finance, and housing, particularly with regards to digital platforms. Currently, I sort my research interests into three categories:

    I. platform real estate.
    Following research from geographer Joe Shaw, my dissertation research explores the spatialization of digital real estate platforms. That is, how do new technologies for the management, transaction, surveyance and surveillance of real estate transform how we come to own the earth—and, perhaps more importantly, who gets to own it?
    II. short-term rentals.
    I have longstanding interests in short-term rental platforms (e.g., Airbnb) and their relationships to the lived experiences of gentrification. Drawing on contemporary writing on platform urbanism, my research into short-term rentals shows how people come to experience feelings of displacement while remaining "in place"—and why that's actually good for platforms like Airbnb.
    III. sweat.
    Inspired by the Sweat Geographies Collective, I've recently become fascinated with how sweat has historically and geographically emerged as an object of medical inquiry, especially within the context of Western and colonial medicine. Current trends in sweat sensing technology, which blur the lines between medical devices and consumer goods, call into question: how does sweat "matter" in the age of ubiquitous computation?

creative writing



Check out Blotterature Literary Magazine

Sometimes, I write essays and short stories.

I mostly write creative nonfiction, especially memoir-type stuff. In it, I try to explore the complicated relationships that people (especially me) have with place. My writing's been published in Blotterature Literary Magazine.

My fiction, especially in collaboration with Robby Hardesty, focuses on the adventures and exploits of The Editors. We have no control over when it shall be published; rather, we lay in waiting, anxiously wondering when it will publish us.
